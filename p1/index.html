The sun and all the planets of our solar system began as a giant cloud of molecular gas and dust.

About 4.57 billion years ago, something happened that caused the cloud to collapse.

Eventually, gravitational pressure caused the material at the center to become so hot it initiated nuclear fusion.

Nuclear fusion continues as matter is converted into radiant energy.

So far, the sun has converted about 100 times Earth's mass into energy

As the sun rapidly burns through the last of its hydrogen reserves it will have expanded over 10 times its size and 50 times its brightness consuming the inner planets.

Eventually the fusion process will destabilize the sun and after several thermal pulses, it will eject over half its mass creating a planetary nebula with a white dwarf at the center surviving for trillions of years.
